Bagging

  • Here are the proper steps for bagging.
  • Attach bags to the line
  • Grab one of each of your parts (locknut, plain, stud and a cushion) and put it on your desk for inspection.
  • Once your parts are inspected properly by Your team lead you can load them into the dumper and put them on your table
  • Grab your inspected bag that has all its proper components and take them back to the high-speed scale to get your nominal weight.
  • Set the scale, accordingly, using the locknut to gauge your high and low on the scale.
  • Once you start running, attach your plastic trim to the plastic rewinder.
  • Make sure to hit your green start button to ensure your numbers are being recorded properly.
  • Verify that your boxer has set the scale and is ready to run
  • Have constant communication with your boxer so they can help with any rejected parts,(These will be the bags that the scale rejects) reducing the stop of production to fix the bags.
  • Anytime you run out of clamps, let your boxer or lead know that more need to be loaded
  • Once you complete the order, scan the vorne paperwork to setup the next order
  • When finished, place the work order in the black basket on the leads desk so that they can report the work.
  • Make sure you are writing down how many changeovers and all the parts you produced throughout the day.

    Boxing

    Boxers have to be in cohesion with their bagger in order to keep a line running consistently. Here are some steps to follow to ensure this happens.
  • Make sure the line is always running with boxes filled at each conveyer
  • Keep the dumpers filled with clamps
  • Take care of the scrap plastic from the re-winders
  • Be alert for anything the bagger may need.
  • Help gather boxes of bags to fill the order
  • Take back material to the correct location
  • Stack skids correctly
  • Make sure your scale is set and the qty is exact.
  • Garbage should be taken out daily.
  • Make sure to stay ahead on the staged orders.

    Team Lead

    Being a part of every of every change over Change overs can be done by dividing the amount of time spent on the floor, by using the time management formula (Priority = Importance X Urgency). Being a part of a changeover is the most urgent occurrence on the floor on a regular basis. The priority of the bagging lead should be ensuring that the bagger has what they need to produce. Maintaining equipment Minimal and or Maximum maintenance to be done on the lines to keep them running. Reporting the work orders and verfiying the components were properly transfered to the lines is vital to the operational flow of the bagiing department. Leadership Responsibilities There must be direct communication from meetings to team. Giving at least 15 min a day of instruction and feedback from the leadership meetings. The bagging lead should consistently be Identifying and creating solutions for future efficiency to ensure a better operation overall.

    Material Handlers

    The material handler is almost like the oil that keeps the bagging engine running smoothly. They are to keep the sprint baggers staged by making sure the components are transfered to the bagging lines in the gun as well as physically and verifying the correct qty is transfered to the lines.
